Our joined body then was his


Quite contradict'ry tragedy and time
Now seem, for one begins and time now lacks
A means to still proceed. You can't relax
Without the fear this is the paradigm
By which we live: where life itself can steal
To end some timeless struggle understood
By only them who've spilt their precious blood.
It seems we all are pawns of this surreal
Phenomenon—not conservation just
Of mass and energy, emotion too,
For all the tears of all his friends and you
Were once contained within just him, our guest
Who's now relieved his noble struggle to
Protect from those he loved the hurt within...
So much I wish he'd seen that friends and kin
Would surely help his pains from mind dequeue.

I hate that when we've scapegoats quick we blame
But when we have ourselves, we all dissolve
The guilt inside with helpless hope to solve
What turmoil ment'ly lurks without a name.

This man's decision, his to make, is real.
Now all us individ'ly must decide
To bleed or heal and cope with suicide.
But when the days that slowly pass just feel
Absurd, we must remember all our friends
And hope together we can make some sense
Of this. To love, to hurt without defense,
To wonder why–with help the darkness ends
For all are filled with pieces come from him.
Alone these pieces might of sadness be,
But brought together by us all we'll see
That when a great man dies, from mind and limb
His soul's deprived. Yet when in mem'ry came
Us all to congregate, I saw that our
Joined body then was his for one last hour.
In friendly minds and souls he died in fame.

To love now's more incentive, twofold gain:
To be the force which holds the pieces tight
Of all our loved ones buried out of sight,
And to ensure our living friends remain.
